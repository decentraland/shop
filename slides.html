The buyer never sees crypto

  • Log in with an email

  • Every price fixed in Credits
  • One click — no wallet, no gas, no signature

MANA is still there — just moved into the plumbing.

03 · economic model

The keys

  1. Credits — fixed price in USD (1 credit = $0.10)
  2. Items — fixed USD, shown as Credits
  3. Liquidity expressed in Credits
  4. card → USDC → (at spend) MANA → settle
  5. MANA stays the settlement token — audited contract reused
  6. Email wallet, gasless · assets self-custody, currency custodial
  7. Creators earn MANA — hold or cash out

05 · why it's safe

We hold dollars, not MANA

Convert to MANA only at the instant of each sale — buying exactly what that sale needs, at that moment's price.

Day 1 · MANA $0.66

$1 item → buy 1.5 MANA → settle

Day 30 · MANA $0.33

$1 item → buy 3 MANA → settle

Never hold MANA over time → MANA's price can't hurt us. It self-balances.

06 · mana economics

Volume, not net demand

we buy +1.5→ creator sells −1.46= net ≈ the fee

Net-new demand = what's retained: fees kept in MANA + creators who hold. No burn for now.

Still strictly better than a design with no MANA at all.

08 · architecture

No new marketplace contract

Settlement is the audited CreditsManagerPolygon — it already custodies MANA and calls accept([...]), several items in one tx.

Treasury (MPC)→ credits-signer→ relayer→ CreditsManager

New work is off-chain: ledger, oracle-priced signing, USDC→MANA swap, Stripe. Buyer signs nothing.

09 · migration

Bring your listings to the Shop

log in→ fetch old MANA listings→ re-price to Credits→ sign new + cancel old

Liquidity moves over; the seller's listings are now stable in Credits. Offchain re-pricing is gasless.
